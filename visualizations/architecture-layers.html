Decision Intelligence Architecture

A multi-layer operating system for AI-native organizations

Constitutional Layer

Foundation

The authoritative layer that owns IP, principles, and the conceptual substrate. Provides structural governance through licensing, not equity.

Owns

  • Core substrate IP
  • Foundational ontologies
  • Stewardship frameworks
  • Meta-architecture

Does Not Own

  • Equity in companies
  • Revenue streams
  • Customer data
  • Operational control

Key Principle

  • Structural authority via IP and contracts
  • Never through equity ownership

Stewardship Layer

Advisory

Portfolio-level intelligence and advisory oversight. Observes, contextualizes, and informs‚Äîbut does not operate or control.

Does

  • Maintains coherence
  • Ingests health signals
  • Surfaces strategic insight
  • Proposes, never mandates

Does Not

  • Own equity
  • Execute strategy
  • Manage companies
  • Control capital

Key Principle

  • Advisory by design
  • Non-owning intelligence

Capital Layer

Participation

Ownership, risk, and financial participation. Invests and participates without governing or controlling operations.

Does

  • Structure equity participation
  • Deploy capital to thesis
  • Accept external investors
  • Capture financial upside

Does Not

  • Govern companies
  • Operate teams
  • Override stewardship
  • Control execution

Key Principle

  • Participates, never controls
  • Only entity on cap tables

Substrate Layer

Infrastructure

Market-specific operating systems with bounded ontologies. Each substrate is a projection of the shared conceptual spine.

Properties

  • Domain-specific schemas
  • Bounded ontologies
  • Decision primitives
  • Authority structures

Examples

  • Enterprise (B2G)
  • Business (B2B)
  • Consumer (B2C)

Key Principle

  • Projections, not copies
  • Clean translation boundaries

Operating Layer

Execution

Fully autonomous companies that execute, learn, and evolve. Each company is responsible for its own decisions, data, and outcomes.

Owns

  • Product IP
  • Customer relationships
  • Operational data
  • Revenue and P&L

Operates

  • Independently governed
  • Different markets OK
  • Different speeds OK
  • Can exit or sunset

Key Principle

  • No single failure destabilizes whole
  • Autonomy with alignment
